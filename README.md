Banking Customer Analytics & Churn Risk Insights
 Project Overview

Customer retention is a critical challenge for retail banks, as even a small increase in churn can result in significant revenue loss.
This project focuses on analyzing customer behavior and identifying churn risk indicators using exploratory and diagnostic analytics, similar to real-world banking consulting engagements.

The objective is to provide explainable, data-driven insights that help banks proactively identify at-risk customers and improve retention strategies.

1. Business Objectives

Understand key demographic and behavioral factors influencing customer churn

Identify high-risk and high-value customer segments

Support data-driven customer retention and engagement strategies

2. Dataset Description

The dataset contains customer-level information related to:

Demographics (Age, Gender, Geography)

Account details (Balance, Credit Score, Tenure)

Product usage and engagement

Churn status (Exited)

Each row represents a unique banking customer.

3. Tools & Technologies

Python

Pandas, NumPy

Matplotlib, Seaborn

Jupyter Notebook

4. Analysis Approach

Data Cleaning & Preparation

Removed irrelevant identifiers and PII fields

Handled outliers using business-appropriate capping

Engineered meaningful features for segmentation

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Analyzed churn trends across demographics, geography, and behavior

Examined the impact of activity status, balance, and product usage on churn

Churn Risk Indicators & Segmentation

Developed explainable, rule-based churn risk flags

Segmented customers based on risk and value to identify priority retention groups

Insights & Recommendations

Summarized key churn drivers

Proposed actionable, business-focused retention strategies

5. Key Insights

Inactive customers exhibit significantly higher churn rates

Customers with fewer banking products are more likely to churn

Certain regions experience consistently higher churn

High-value customers are not immune to churn when engagement declines

Simple, explainable risk indicators effectively identify churn-prone segments

6. Business Recommendations

Implement early-warning systems for inactive customers

Prioritize retention efforts for high-value, high-risk customers

Encourage multi-product adoption through cross-selling strategies

Develop region-specific and age-segmented engagement initiatives

7. Note on Modeling

This project intentionally focuses on exploratory and diagnostic analytics rather than predictive modeling.
The emphasis is on explainable insights and business decision support, which are often the first and most critical steps in real-world banking analytics.
